The Ligue 1:

France’s Ligue de Football Professionel declared the Ligue 1 and 2 seasons over on Thursday, days after the government said major sporting events would be banned until September.

Paris Saint-Germain, who were 12 points ahead of Marseille when season was suspended in mid-March due to the Covid-19 outbreak, have been declared Ligue 1 champions.

PSG boss Nasser Al-Khelaifi said his club was dedicating its ninth league win to "all the healthcare workers and other everyday heroes who have earned all our admiration with their commitment and self-sacrifice for long weeks."

Season Restart:

The French prime minister, Edouard Philippe, has announced “big sporting affairs” including professional football cannot return in the country until September and the 2019-20 season in Ligue 1 and 2 will therefore end immediately.

Matches in France have been suspended since the start of March because of the coronavirus pandemic, with several players having agreed to defer up to 50% of their salaries to help their clubs through the crisis after broadcasters initially withheld TV rights.

The decision of abandoing the French Ligue 1 has been confrimed and thus PSG have been crowned as the Champions with Kylian Mbappe been awarded the Golden boot for the most goals scored in the 2019-20 Season.
